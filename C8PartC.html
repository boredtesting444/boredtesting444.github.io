Complete the statement below regarding some of the desired outcomes of applying different cooking methods to dry goods: 

 

Baking: Ideal for dry produce like flour, grains, nuts, and seeds, it  toasts  and crisps up ingredients while enhancing their natural flavours.

Baking: Dry heat method for meat, making the outside  crispy  and the inside soft. It also creates new flavours through browning and chemical changes.

Poaching: Gentle cooking method for delicate meats in flavoured liquid, keeping them moist and tender.

Roasting: Dry heat method for meat, creating a crusty  exterior  and juicy interior with  caramelised  flavours.

Shallow frying: Cooking meat with a small amount of oil for a crispy exterior and juicy interior.

Sous vide: Gentle cooking method to maintain natural flavours and juices, with  consistent  doneness.

Steaming: Gentle cooking method to keep meat moist, tender, and  preserve  natural flavours.

Stewing: Wet heat method for tougher cuts of meat, making them tender and flavourful in slow-cooked dishes like stews and curries.

The list below is culinary terms that relate to grilling food.

Match the terms to the descriptions.

 

 Sear : The process of cooking the surface of the meat quickly over high heat to create a crust and seal in the juices.

 Baste : Brushing or spooning liquid, such as a marinade or melted butter, over the food while grilling to keep it moist and add flavour.

 Flareups : Flames that erupt from the grill when fat or oil drips onto the heat source.

 Grill marks : The dark, charred lines that are left on the surface of the food from contact with the hot grates.
