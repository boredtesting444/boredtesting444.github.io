Fill the Gaps:
Complete the statement below regarding some of the desired outcomes of applying different cooking methods to dairy products:  

 

Baking: Dry heat method for creating crispy crusts or golden-brown tops on dishes like  quiches  or casseroles with dairy products.

Boiling: Wet heat method not typically used with dairy due to  curdling , but milk and cream can be heated for custards and sauces.

Deep frying: Submerging breaded cheese in hot oil for crispy, golden results, popular with  mozzarella  sticks or cheese curds.

Grilling:  Dry  heat method melting cheese on the grill creating crispy crusts.

Roasting: Dry heat method for  crispy  exteriors and tender interiors, ideal for dishes like macaroni or cauliflower cheese.

Shallow frying: Frying dairy products in a small amount of  oil  until crispy and golden, commonly used with Halloumi cheese.

Stewing: Wet heat method suitable for dairy  dishes  with cream or milk-based sauces like Paneer.
